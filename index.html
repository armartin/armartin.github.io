Human population and statistical genetics

I am a postdoctoral research fellow in Mark Dalyâ€™s lab at the Analytic & Translational Genetics Unit in Massachusetts General Hospital as well as the Stanley Center for Psychiatric Research at the Broad Institute. As a population and statistical geneticist, my research investigates how human history shapes global genetic and phenotypic diversity, and the the role these forces play in predicting complex disease risk and evolutionarily important traits. I am also particularly interested in approaches for reducing health disparities caused by vast Eurocentric genetic study biases.
